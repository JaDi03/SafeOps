## 29 CFR 1910.132 — General Requirements for PPE
**Title:** Personal Protective Equipment (General Requirements)
**Requirement:** Employers must assess workplace hazards and provide appropriate PPE.
**Common Violations:** Workers without hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, or high-visibility vests in required areas.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625 per violation.
**Penalty (Willful):** Up to $156,259 per violation.

## 29 CFR 1910.133 — Eye and Face Protection
**Requirement:** Eye/face protection required where there is risk of injury from flying particles, molten metal, chemicals, or radiation.
**Common Violations:** Missing safety glasses near grinders, welding without proper shields.

## 29 CFR 1910.134 — Respiratory Protection
**Requirement:** Respirators required when engineering controls are not feasible for airborne hazards.
**Common Violations:** Missing respirators in dusty or chemical environments.

## 29 CFR 1910.135 — Head Protection
**Requirement:** Head protection (hard hats) required in areas with risk of head injury from falling objects.
**Common Violations:** Workers in construction/industrial zones without hard hats.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625 per instance.

## 29 CFR 1910.147 — Control of Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout)
**Title:** LOTO — Lockout/Tagout
**Requirement:** Procedures to disable machinery during maintenance to prevent unexpected startup.
**Common Violations:** Machine maintenance without proper lockout procedures, missing tags.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625 per violation.
**Why Critical:** Leading cause of industrial fatalities. ~3 million workers at risk.

## 29 CFR 1910.157 — Portable Fire Extinguishers
**Requirement:** Fire extinguishers must be maintained, accessible, and employees must be trained.
**Common Violations:** Blocked extinguisher access, expired equipment, missing inspections.

## 29 CFR 1910.176 — Handling Materials (General)
**Requirement:** Safe storage, stacking, and material handling to prevent injuries.
**Common Violations:** Unstable stacking, blocked aisles, improper lifting.

## 29 CFR 1910.178 — Powered Industrial Trucks (Forklifts)
**Title:** Forklift Safety
**Requirement:** Only trained and certified operators. Speed limits, load capacity, pedestrian awareness.
**Common Violations:** Untrained operators, excessive speed, carrying passengers, obstructed view, operating near pedestrians without warning.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625 per violation.
**Why Critical:** ~85 forklift fatalities/year in the US. ~35,000 serious injuries.

## 29 CFR 1910.212 — General Requirements for Machines
**Title:** Machine Guarding
**Requirement:** Guards required on machines with moving parts that could cause injury.
**Common Violations:** Missing guards on conveyor belts, press brakes, saws.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625.

## 29 CFR 1910.303-308 — Electrical Safety
**Requirement:** Electrical equipment must be properly installed, grounded, and maintained.
**Common Violations:** Exposed wiring, overloaded circuits, missing ground fault protection.

## 29 CFR 1910.22 — Walking-Working Surfaces
**Requirement:** Floors must be clean, dry, and free of hazards. Spills must be addressed immediately.
**Common Violations:** Wet floors without signs, cluttered walkways, uneven surfaces.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625.

## 29 CFR 1926.501 — Fall Protection
**Title:** Duty to Have Fall Protection
**Requirement:** Protection required at heights of 6 feet or more in construction.
**Common Violations:** Missing guardrails, no harness at height, unprotected edges.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625.
**Why Critical:** Falls are the #1 cause of construction deaths.

## 29 CFR 1910.36-37 — Means of Egress (Emergency Exits)
**Requirement:** Exit routes must be kept clear, properly marked, and illuminated.
**Common Violations:** Blocked emergency exits, missing EXIT signs, locked doors.
**Penalty (Serious):** $15,625.

## 29 CFR 1910.1200 — Hazard Communication (HazCom)
**Requirement:** Chemical hazards must be communicated via labels, SDS, and training.
**Common Violations:** Unlabeled containers, missing Safety Data Sheets, no training.

## 29 CFR 1910.95 — Occupational Noise Exposure
**Requirement:** Hearing protection required when noise exceeds 85 dB TWA.
**Common Violations:** No hearing protection in noisy environments, missing audiometric testing.
